Abstract

The UBI-ID system is a state-aligned economic framework that allocates Universal Basic Income based on legal identity, verified education, social contribution, and criminal record, grounded in the 1958 Constitution of France.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age 18+
  • Digital ID linked to national registry
  • Base UBI: 1,400 EUR/month
  • Minimum threshold: 800 EUR/month

Education Bonus

Only accredited diplomas issued by recognized national institutions are accepted.

LevelBonus
High School+5%
Bachelor+15%
Master+25%
PhD+35%

Contribution Bonus

Verified activities (volunteering, health service, digital commons) provide up to +20% bonus.

Penalty Model

By Sentence Length

  • < 6 months: −5%
  • 6 months–2 years: −15%
  • 2–5 years: −25%
  • 5–10 years: −40%
  • > 10 years: −60% (plus no bonuses for 2 years)

By Offense Type

  • Contravention: −5%
  • Délit: −15%
  • Grave Délit: −25%
  • Crime: −40%
  • Constitutional Offense: −60%

Final Calculation


Final_UBI = Base_UBI × (1 + EducationBonus + ContributionBonus − PenaltyFactor)

Constraints: Bonus ≤ +60%, Penalty ≤ −60%, Min UBI = 800 EUR/month

Legal Basis

Rooted in French Constitution (1958), Articles 1, 34, 55, 66, 68. See data/constitution_1958.txt.
